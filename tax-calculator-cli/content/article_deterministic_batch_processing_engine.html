Designing a Deterministic Batch Processing Engine

Author: Roman Vidayko · Date: 2026-02-09

Copyright © 2025 Roman Vidayko

A reliable batch processing engine is essential for systems that must evaluate large volumes of ordered transactions with precision and repeatability. Such a tool typically consumes structured input line by line, where each line represents an independent batch. Operations are processed sequentially to preserve temporal correctness, while internal state, such as accumulated quantities, rolling averages, and carried-over adjustments is maintained only for the duration of a single batch and then fully reset.


One of the core challenges in batch computation is managing cumulative logic. Values derived from earlier operations often influence later results, requiring careful handling of weighted calculations and historical offsets. Negative outcomes may need to be preserved and applied against future positives, while threshold-based rules can selectively suppress outputs without discarding their effect on internal state.


Accuracy is paramount. Fixed-precision arithmetic and explicit rounding rules avoid drift and ensure consistent results. A minimal interface, clear separation of concerns, and strong test coverage complete the design.
